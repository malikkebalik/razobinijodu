Spice Relationships and Kids

Grace Latoya Hamilton was born in Spanish Town, St. Catherine, Jamaica. Unfortunately, she experienced the loss of her father at the tender age of nine when she was growing up in Portmore.

During her childhood, she faithfully attended church and took charge of leading the choir.

Spice’s formative years also included a period in Finsbury Park, London, where she resided with her grandparents and pursued her education before returning to Jamaica.

While studying at St. Catherine High School, she actively engaged in the Jamaica Cultural Development Commission (JCDC) festival, showcasing her musical talents and receiving numerous accolades.

Despite her initial aspirations of becoming a chartered accountant, her passion for music led her to embark on a career in the industry.

She enrolled at the Edna Manley College of the Visual and Performing Arts to further develop her musical and dramatic skills.

During her college years, the artist ventured into dancehall music and gained local recognition for her captivating performances at nearby stage shows.

In 2009, she engaged with Nicholas Lall, her partner since 2006. However, their engagement was called off, and their relationship ended in 2016.

Spice and Nicholas Lall share the joy of parenthood, with a son born in 2007 and a daughter welcomed into the world in 2011.

In 2020, her romantic path led her to a relationship with Justin Budd, an American cinematographer she met in Atlanta. Unfortunately, their journey together concluded in 2022.

Spice ‘Grace Layota Hamilton’ Net Worth

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Grace Latoya Hamilton, popularly known as Spice, is a Jamaican dancehall artist, singer, and songwriter, boasting an impressive net worth of $5 million as of 2023.

She catapulted to stardom in the 2000s with infectious hits like “Romping Shop” and “So Mi Like It,” cementing her status as a force to be reckoned with in the vibrant dancehall scene.

Recognized for her unparalleled talent and impact, Spice has rightfully crowned the “Queen of Dancehall” in 2020, solidifying her reign as a true music royalty.

Breaking new ground, Spice unleashed her much-anticipated debut studio album, “10,” in 2021, captivating listeners and critics alike, ultimately earning her a coveted Grammy Award nomination.

Displaying her entrepreneurial flair, Spice ventured into diverse business ventures, including launching Spicey Couture, a clothing company, in 2009. However, as time unfolded, the company gracefully concluded its journey by 2020.

Embracing her enterprising spirit, Spice boldly opened a luxurious beauty salon in the heart of Kingston, Jamaica, in 2010 and subsequently delighted patrons by establishing the trendy sports bar 8 Ball in Portmore.
