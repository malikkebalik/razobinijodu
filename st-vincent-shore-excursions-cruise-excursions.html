Scenic, Adventurous St. Vincent Shore Excursions

St. Vincent and the Grenadines is a lovely chain of islands and a popular destination on a southern Caribbean cruise. While on your St. Vincent cruise, you can explore the main island of St. Vincent or head to some of the smaller islands that make up the Grenadines. St. Vincent shore excursions can be relaxing or adventurous depending on what you choose.

One of the most popular St. Vincent cruise excursions to experience while in the port of Kingstown is to get out onto the water on a sailboat that takes you around the gorgeous coastline of St. Vincent and to a secluded beach where you can swim or just lounge on the sand.

Island drives are other popular St. Vincent shore excursions. During scenic drives around the island, you’ll learn about its history and its present day culture and strong agricultural presence. You’ll also see St. Vincent’s iconic sites, like the uniquely designed Church of the Assumption, the early 19th century Fort Charlotte, and the beautiful Botanic Gardens, which are the oldest in the western hemisphere and date back to 1765.

For some more adventure with your scenery, opt for a hike along the Vermont Nature Trail. The Vermont Nature Trail goes through the lushly green and wildly beautiful Vermont Valley. During the hike, you’ll learn about the various flora and fauna that grows on the island as well as have the chance to see the Amazona Guildingii parrot, which is the national bird and is endemic to this island.

Another Caribbean cruise excursion that will get you right into the heart of nature is one that visits Dark View Falls, an awe-inspiring natural wonder on the island. Dark View Falls features a double waterfall, and the cascading water is a powerful sight to behold.

Focus on the beauty and leave the logistics to us on St. Vincent shore excursions, and get ready for a memorable time in St. Vincent and the Grenadines.
