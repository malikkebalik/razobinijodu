FaZe Clan's Profile, Net Worth, Age, Height, Relationships, FAQs

 Posted on September 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  516 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

On May 30, 2010, FaZe Sniping made its YouTube debut. Three players, Eric “CLipZ” Rivera, Jeff “House Cat” Emann (now known as “Timid”), and Ben “Resistance” Christensen, originally founded the group as a Call of Duty clan. In Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2, the trio developed trick shotting, earning them a reputation for doing so.

When Ted “Fakie” joined the Clan, their YouTube presence exploded. At that time, FaZe debuted ILLCAMS, the most watched show on their channel. In contrast to other Call of Duty channels, which were concentrated on winning tournaments, FaZe Clan’s focus on trick shotting, and a personality/social media approach led to FaZe Clan becoming one of the first well-known YouTube video game channels.

“FaZe Sniping Intro” is the first video on FaZe Clan’s channel. They introduce themselves as FaZe Sniping, as they previously went by that name.

The channel had one million subscribers in 2012, and the Clan started to participate in esports events. Teams using the FaZe name started taking part in events like the Call of Duty Championship and Counter-Strike: Global Offensive Major Championships, which were independent of the Clan’s YouTube content producers, dividing its members into two largely independent camps.

Although FaZe Clan did not initially have a corporate structure, Thomas “Temperrr” Oliveira, a Brazilian who had been a member of FaZe since he was 16 years old, served as the organisation’s first CEO. In Plainview, New York, Oliveira and COO Richard “Banks” Bengston established the first FaZe shared home in 2014, so they could mix gaming content with lifestyle content.

Sebastian Guerts, a Norwegian businessman who founded the former social media platform Hubrick, decided to invest in FaZe Clan in 2015. Lee Trink, a former record executive, was hired by Hubrick to be his CEO.

International Counter-Strike: Global Offensive players from G2 Esports were acquired by FaZe Clan in January 2016, and they represented FaZe Clan at the 2016 MLG Major Championship: Columbus. The group finished second in ELEAGUE Major: Boston 2018, finishing behind US-based Cloud9.

In 2017, FaZe Clan finished second in the ESL Pro League Season 6 and second in the ESL Pro League Season 7 and ESL One Cologne, respectively. In 2017, FaZe Clan won the ELeague CS: GO Premier championship and $500,000 in prize money.

Greg Selkoe, the creator of streetwear online retailer Karmaloop, took over as FaZe Clan president in 2018, and CEO Lee Trink transitioned into the CEO position full-time.

The first time FaZe entered PlayerUnknown’s Battlegrounds was on November 10, 2017, during the game’s peak time. They sponsored streamers in addition to investing in an esports team.

FaZe jumped on board as soon as it was apparent that Fortnite Battle Royale was the most popular game in the world. They acquired a team that would compete but would primarily stream. The FaZe Fortnite name became synonymous with Turner “Tfue” Tenney.

After acquiring Tass, a former player for Team Dignitas and Hashtag United F.C., FaZe Clan entered FIFA on January 24, 2018. His first competition with the organisation began on January 26, 2018, with the FIFA eWorld Cup in Barcelona.
